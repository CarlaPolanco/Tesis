byHello, Nicholas Copernicus was a mathematician, astronomer, bishop, physicist, governor, jurist and more of the 16th century. He was born in Royal Prussia, Poland and developed a heliocentric model, different from the geocentric that was used at that time, and took astronomy from something more philosophical to something more scientific and gave him mathematical formulation. Per for fear of criticism he did not publish his work until 1543, year in which he died because he was already sick. He dedicated his book to the Pope for the realization of better, safer calendars. His system was not adapted by many scientists and decades laterIt was banned by the Church with the trials of Galileo Galedei and Giordano Bruno.
